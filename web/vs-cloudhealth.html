The CloudHealth complaints we keep hearing

Pricing
"The price went up 3x after the Broadcom acquisition."

CloudHealth was already enterprise-tier pricing. Post-acquisition, teams report 200–400% increases at renewal. nable starts free and scales at $29/seat.

Onboarding
"We waited 6 weeks for our onboarding call."

nable is self-serve. pip install finops-mcp and you're querying cost data in 5 minutes.

Usability
"I have to build dashboards just to answer a simple question."

nable answers cost questions in plain English inside Claude or Cursor. No dashboard to configure, no widget to drag.

Data control
"Our security team won't approve sending billing data to a third party."

With nable, nothing leaves your machine. Queries go direct from your laptop to your cloud provider APIs. Credentials in your OS keyring. Nothing on our servers to breach.

Action
"It shows me the problem but I still have to file the ticket myself."

nable auto-creates the Jira, Linear, or GitHub issue — with the instance ID, current vs recommended type, and projected savings pre-filled.

SaaS blind spots
"CloudHealth doesn't see my Datadog or Snowflake spend."

nable connects 13 providers including Datadog, Snowflake, Stripe, MongoDB Atlas, Twilio, Cloudflare, and Vercel — in the same normalized view as your cloud spend.
