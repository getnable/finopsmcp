nable vs Vantage

Vantage is a hosted cost platform with a polished dashboard and, more recently, an MCP server on top of it. nable is the opposite architecture: it runs locally, your bill never leaves your machine, you start with one command free, and it works inside your editor. If you want a shared web dashboard for a finance team, Vantage is strong. If you are an engineer who wants cost answers and fixes where you already work, without piping your bill into a vendor, that is nable.

When Vantage is the right call

You want a shared, hosted dashboard a finance team logs into, mature cost reports and views, and you are comfortable with your billing data living in a vendor's cloud. Vantage is a well-built platform for that.

When nable fits better

You are an engineer or a small AI-native team who wants cost answers and fixes inside Claude or Cursor, want to start free in one command, care that your bill never leaves your machine, and want cloud and AI/LLM spend in one place with the fix drafted as a PR.

Try nable in 30 seconds, free, no signup. It runs on your machine, reads your cloud and AI bill read-only, answers cost questions in Claude or Cursor, and drafts the fix as a pull request you approve. Nothing leaves your laptop.

Common questions

Is nable a Vantage alternative?

Yes, for the engineer-facing use case. Both answer cross-cloud cost questions. nable differs by running locally (your bill never reaches a vendor), installing with one free command, living in your editor via MCP, covering AI/LLM token spend, and drafting fixes as pull requests. Vantage is a hosted platform better suited to a shared finance dashboard.

Does nable send my billing data anywhere like Vantage does?

No. nable runs on your machine. Credentials stay in your OS keychain and your bill is cached locally; there is no nable backend in the path. This is the core architectural difference from a hosted platform.

Is nable free?

Yes for solo local use, installed with uvx nable. Paid tiers add hosted and team features. There is no signup to try it.

Can nable track OpenAI and Anthropic token costs?

Yes. nable reads OpenAI, Anthropic, and Bedrock usage and splits token cost by model, in the same bill as your cloud spend, so you see AI and cloud together.
